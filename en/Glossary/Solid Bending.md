# Specialization in Kinetic Magic

Although **Kinetic Magic** can technically act on any mass with inertia, the molecular complexity and structural cohesion make the execution of "bending" radically different depending on the target material. For this reason, most practitioners opt for a **domain specialization**, focusing their training and mana consumption on a single state or type of matter to reach levels of mastery unattainable by generalists.

---

# I. The Constraint of Molecular Complexity

The effectiveness of a kinetic spell depends on the magician's ability to "feel" and manipulate the vectors acting on the particles of the material:

* **Low Resistance (Gas):** Requires an ability to see the big picture and control large areas. The specialization here focuses on the management of flows (e.g. air currents, atmospheric pressure).
* **Medium Strength (Liquids):** Requires precision control of density and viscosity. Magicians specializing in liquids are often employed in industrial support or sabotage contexts.
* **High Strength (Solids):** Requires extreme energy expenditure and a deep understanding of the atomic structure of the solid (e.g. metallic bonds, crystalline structures).

---

# II. Because Specialization is the Standard

Specialization is not a choice of style, but a logical necessity dictated by three critical factors:

1. **Mana Optimization:** The magician's brain and soul must "learn" the rate of vibration of the material. Specializing means drastically reducing the mana wasted to overcome the resistances of the chosen material.
2. **Psychic Affinity:** Over time, the bond between the mage's mind and the material becomes symbiotic. A metal expert senses the tension of a steel beam before even touching it, an insight that would not be possible by dividing one's attention among too many different materials.
3. **Combat Efficiency:** In the chaos of battle, there is no time to analyze the physical properties of an object. The magician must be able to "perform" the movement instantly; the muscular and psychic memory of the specialized material makes the reaction almost subconscious.

---

# III. The Hierarchy of Competencies

Specialists are generally divided according to their area of ​​expertise:

* **Pneumatics (Gas Specialists):** Masters of atmospheres, capable of creating air pockets to suffocate or accelerate projectiles.
* **Plumbers (Fluid Specialists):** Fluid manipulators, essential for handling chemicals or "wave" attacks.
* **Geomancers/Metallokinetics (Solid Specialists):** The most feared in the field. Capable of bending armor, dismembering structures, or turning terrain into a weapon. Their mastery is considered the pinnacle of kinetic discipline.

# IV. Training Status
In the more advanced Guilds, training begins not with magic, but with applied physics. A potential Master must understand the molecular nature of their material before attempting the first conversion of ethereal energy. This scientific basis ensures that, once specialized, the Questar is not just a user of force, but an engineer of the movement of matter.
