#Argus

**Argus** was the proprietary technological system developed and employed by [[en/Cast/Groups/Olympus Foundation/Olympus Foundation|Olympus Foundation]] between 2007 and 2024. The system specialized in instant physical enhancement through the controlled administration of fast-acting chemical preparations directly into the user's bloodstream.

---

# I. Technical Operation

The Argus system stood out for its modular nature and immediacy of application:

* **Administration Interface:** The equipment consisted of ergonomic tactical armbands or belts, designed to be worn under uniform or body armor.
* **Injection Mechanism:** Each device was equipped with a high precision **retractable needle**, capable of piercing technical fabrics and skin in a fraction of a second, ensuring the administration of the compound.
* **Charging Port:** The devices had a dedicated slot where **special capsules** were inserted. These contained pre-mixed chemical reagent, vacuum sealed to prevent deterioration or oxidation of the active agents.

---

# II. Tactical Application

The Argus system allowed Olympus Foundation agents to alter their physical abilities in situations of extreme stress:

* **Quick Answer:** Thanks to direct injection, the effects of the compounds (such as increased strength, speed of reflexes or accelerated regeneration) occurred almost instantly.
* **Dosage Management:** The system was able to monitor the user's heartbeat, allowing the belt or cuff to adjust the injection pressure to avoid vascular trauma.

---

# III. Legacy and Disposal

Used for nearly two decades, Argus was the mainstay of Olympus operatives' physical efficiency before being gradually overtaken by new augmentation methodologies, likely tied to magic or more advanced technologies that don't depend on the constant injection of chemical compounds.

> ### ── Archive Note ──
> *«The Argus system was not just an injection method; it was the symbol of an era in which the Olympus Foundation sought to dominate the human body through chemistry. When you hear the click of the needle, you know your body is about to stop being just human.”* — Excerpt from Olympus operatives' training manuals (circa 2012).

---

# IV. Current Status

As of 2024, the system is considered obsolete and largely decommissioned. While remnant units still exist in warehouses or in the hands of collectors and mercenaries, production of the compatible pods has ceased, making the Argus devices little more than relics of a bygone era of augmentation engineering.
