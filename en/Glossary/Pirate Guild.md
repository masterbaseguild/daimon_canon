# Pirate Guild

The **Pirate Guild** is a subcategory of the [[en/Glossary/Gilde|guilds]], defined not so much by a uniform internal structure, but by its intrinsically hostile and predatory *modus operandi* towards any other entity operating in the Void. Often made up of [[en/Glossary/Quester|Questors]] declared outlaws, the pirate guild acts outside of any jurisdiction, turning exploration into an act of aggression.

---

# I. Operational Dynamics and Crime

Unlike trade or research guilds, pirate guilds base their economy on a parasitic system of resource accumulation:

* **Illicit Activities:** The core business consists of assaults on merchant convoys, hijackings of transport ships, extortion of isolated outposts and looting of shipwrecks or vulnerable installations.
* **Outlaw Status:** Most Pirate Guilds operate under arrest warrants in one or more sectors. This forces them into constant mobility and survival logistics based on mobile bases or unsupervised space sectors.
* **Vagueness of Appellation:** The term "Pirate Guild" is extremely vague. It is often used by central governments and large corporations as a derogatory term to delegitimize any group of Questors who refuse to obey their directives, making it difficult to distinguish between common criminals and ideological rebels.

---

# II. Codes and Vocations (Beyond Simple Looting)

Despite their reputation as unscrupulous criminals, many Pirate Guilds hide more complex realities behind their label:

* **Codes of Conduct:** Some guilds operate under strict internal codes of honor that govern the division of loot, the treatment of prisoners, or engagement in combat, distancing themselves from the chaos of lawless marauders.
* **Specific Vocations:** Some formations are born with political or philosophical intentions, using piracy as a tool of guerrilla warfare against dominant government or corporate entities. In these cases, the assault is not dictated only by profit, but by sabotage aimed at damaging the economic flows of a political rival.

---

# III. Social and Strategic Impact

The proliferation of Pirate Guilds is a direct symptom of the fragility of state control in the MasterVerse:

1. **The Challenge to the Trade Houses:** The Pirate Guilds represent the constant threat to the trade routes of large corporations, forcing the latter to invest massively in armed escorts.
2. **Refuge for the Dissenters:** For many individuals who do not recognize themselves in the rigid structures of the MasterBase or the Nations, the Pirate Guild becomes the only possible refuge, attracting brilliant minds but refractory to the authorities.
3. **The Weight of the Myth:** The figure of the "space pirate" occupies an important place in the collective imagination; Often romanticized or demonized depending on your point of view, their actions determine the shipping lanes and safety of less traveled sectors.

---

# IV. Engagement Risks

For a Master, encountering a Pirate Guild in a border area is a high-risk situation. Their unpredictability, combined with the possession of sometimes experimental or recovered technologies (the result of years of looting), makes them tactically difficult adversaries, capable of using the surrounding environment and the dangers of space to their advantage.
