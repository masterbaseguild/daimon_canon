# Starbase

A **Starbase** is a partially or fully self-sustaining mobile space station that serves as an operational headquarters, logistics center, and residence for members of a [[en/Glossary/Guild|guild]]. Born from the need for a permanent presence in a lawless universe, the Starbase represents the symbol of the emancipation of private organizations from the authority of states.

---

# I. Historical Genesis: From Wild Space to the Guild Model

The Starbase phenomenon exploded alongside the technological revolution of the early 21st century.

* **The Catalyst (2001):** The release of the patent for the **Warp Engine** into the public domain lowered barriers to entry, allowing private entities to match the space navigation capabilities of national entities.
* **Strategic Immunity:** In a phase of regulatory vacuum, having an operational base in the Void was, in fact, equivalent to immunity from the judicial system. This gray area allowed the first Guilds to consolidate away from government interference.

---

# II. Architecture and Common Equipment

Although there is no single patent, a high-profile Starbase stands out for a series of essential components that guarantee its autonomy:

* **Warp Engine:** The heart of the system, essential for intersystem (not intergalactic) travel. It gives the base unprecedented tactical mobility.
* **Aether Portal:** The preferred system for individual rapid transportation, allowing Guild members to move between the Starbase and planetary surfaces.
* **Sustenance Department:** Cultivation and farming infrastructure that makes the base a closed ecosystem, drastically reducing the need for external supplies.
* **Living Department:** In addition to the dormitories, it includes recreational areas necessary to maintain the moral cohesion of the members during long missions in the Void.
* **Tactical Hangar:** Space dedicated to the support fleet (shuttles, fighters, drones), crucial for search, defense or incursion operations.
* **Composite Structure:** The body is usually a reinforced architecture of **carbon, Kevlar and titanium** to balance lightness, durability and resistance to space radiation.

---

# III. Analysis of the Phenomenon

> ### ── Archive Note (from Aaron Saava, 2020) ──
> *«The very existence of starbases is nothing more than an inevitable effect of the slow and backward policy that has characterized the last decade. [...] When the states decided to regulate the new paths, the culture of the frontier had already been written.»*

The Starbase is not just a vehicle; it is an extension of the philosophy of the Guild that owns it. Its extreme customization - due to the lack of an industrial standard - allows each organization to shape its headquarters on the specific needs of its missions, and to express its own identity.

---

# IV. Operational Status
Today, Starbases are dynamic entities. Whether they operate as trade centers, defense bases, or [[en/Glossary/Aether|magical research]] mobile laboratories, their presence is the metric by which a Guild's influence in a given sector is measured. Their ability to move quickly across the Warp makes each guild an unpredictable and omnipresent political player.
