"I'm in."

"Good, now take the first corridor on the right."

Alone's voice echoed in Emily's earphone, as she had just snuck into Asgard's new headquarters in Florence.

"Remember the plan: get to the warehouse containing the exoskeleton, then connect the bug to the teleport-pad and I'll do the rest."

"You forgot the part where I have to fight my way to said depot. By the way, here's one, give me a sec."

Unaware of anything, a guard was about to turn the corner, when suddenly he saw the tip of an arrow emerge from behind the wall.

"It's really ironic, they're so busy defending themselves from bullets that paradoxically the most effective way to get them out of the way is to use a bow and arrow," Emily smiled, noticing the bulletproof suit that wrapped the victim's lifeless body.

"Be careful not to get distracted, we have no idea when they'll raise the alarm," Alone scolded her.

"Clear."

After continuing for a few minutes, a sudden noise shook the building.

"What was that?"

"Cover blown," Alone noted.

"Then it's time to change our approach."

Emily pulled out two cans, and sprinted forward.

Further away, two guards alerted by the alarm had noticed his presence and had aimed their rifles. Emily threw the canisters, then vanished, leaving the bullets to embed themselves in the floor.

Once they hit the ground, the canisters shattered, releasing their contents. The guards observed in confusion that it was normal water.

But then, as they advanced, the puddle began to move.

Liquid coils wrapped around them from their feet up to their chest, and then reached their arms.

Then, an impressive force released from them, crushing the two men's wrists. The rifles fell to the ground.

Emily peeked around the corner. The coils had meanwhile solidified, trapping the two unfortunates in a grip of ice.

"Tell your superiors that you could use a refresher course," Emily giggled as she passed between the two guardians, "It's kind of hard to hit someone who moves at superhuman speed."

A few turns later, Alone spoke again, and informed Emily that she had reached her destination.

After blowing the lock, the young Master found herself in a large room, dominated by an imposing humanoid-shaped sarcophagus.

"Is this it?"

"Exactly this. Come on, connect the bug, now it's my turn."

As Alone took control of the farcaster, Emily noticed something imprinted on the side of the stolen goods.

“Hey Alone, do you have any idea who made this exoskeleton?”

"According to sources it should have been built internally at Asgard, why?"

"This is not the Asgard crest. It's very different, it appears to be an X superimposed on a cube... Does it mean anything to you?"

"I don't think I've ever seen a symbol like that even when I was working on Aura. In any case, I'm done, so hop on in and I'll take you home with the objective."

"All right."
