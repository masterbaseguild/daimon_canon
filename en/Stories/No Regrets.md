The Piazza dei Lions was completely deserted that night.

Theo's figure stood out in front of the church facade. He was looking at the dome.

He remembered the time he and PK climbed there after they got their hands on some new grappling hooks. He remembered how PK always beat him when it came to climbing buildings.
He also remembered how Bruno got angry every time the two put themselves in danger for a stupid race. And how Entity persuaded him to let them do it.

Theo bit his lip.

He had decided to return. After everything that had happened, he was back on the team. This time, no longer as leader: that honor and burden had fallen on Entity's shoulders. Theo couldn't decide whether it infuriated him, annoyed him, or left him cold.

However, she couldn't hate him. Because every time he tried to blame him for what had happened, he couldn't help but always come to the same conclusion: if Entity was responsible for it, how could he not be responsible too?

Theo put his hands in his hair. Before his eyes, the Master belt glittered under the dim light of the city streetlights.

He had now made his choice. He would move forward with the MasterBase, should it take off or sink. Either way, he would have been fine. He couldn't decide whether it would hurt more to keep the memory of his companions alive, or to die with it. So, they said, why not let fate decide?

Theo stood up, and turned back to the church. He pulled out his grappling hook, and in a single motion, he was at the top of the dome.

North Regium lay silently under his gaze. Until two years ago, that city could never have been so silent. So it was fine. It was all he needed.
