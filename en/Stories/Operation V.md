"Hey Entity, are you really sure it's a good idea to take a vacation right now?"

"Absolutely, we broke our backs assembling the Base. Everyone needs a break."

Tecmov was perplexed: he had just finished his greatest creation, but he had no possibility of keeping it under observation. He had been forced to hand over its management to the other Masters, and had been dragged back to Aura by Entity, who insisted that a little rest would do him good.

"I don't know if you realize it, but you're constantly thinking about work. You need a break, believe me."

"Maybe you're right, but now is a bit of a dangerous time to do it. The MasterBase is very young, and we're not sure that everything is fixed: an engine malfunction, a broken support beam... I don't think I had enough time for the finishing touches."

"Stories! You're just making excuses because you're obsessed with your inventions."

Yes... Maybe he's right. Tecmov was beginning to believe him.

"Four days in the mountains doesn't seem like the end of the world to me, and when we return, we will be even fresher to continue working."

"Alright, Entity, you win."

Tecmov heaved a sigh, and turned to observe the valley. The view was beautiful from the balcony where they were standing.

"You know," Tecmov continued, "recently I've been noticing something strange in the behavior of that Gondran boy..."

"Garo? You're right, ever since we invited the boys on board he seems very aggressive towards you."

Entity stood up, and walked to the windowsill.

"We've all known him for a short time, but I feel like it's a matter of trust. After all, as of now we live in a Space-Hall designed and built by you; a tremendous amount of trust is required for something like this, and it may be that Garo isn't ready to give it to you yet."

"Does what happened on Iphiridia have anything to do with it?"

"Maybe yes, maybe no. Maybe it's what happened before. In any case, it's just another good reason for us all to take a break and reflect."

"But now you're grist for your own mill."

"You say?"

Tecmov was about to reply dejectedly, but was interrupted by Entity's cell phone. The ringtone was that of the Team.

"Ready Emily?" After about ten seconds with the phone to his ear, his gaze darkened.

Tecmov looked at him apprehensively. He didn't like not being aware of things.

"When should they arrive?" He didn't like the turn the situation was taking either.

"Okay, if there is any other news, write to me IMMEDIATELY."

Entity hung up, and finally spilled the beans. Tecmov hung on his lips.

"Our position has been tracked. An Asgard squadron will attack us tonight."

"But there are civilians too!"

"That's exactly the point. They want to plant a nighttime bomb and blame us..."

"...To further discredit Olympus?"

"Precisely. But just as they found out about us, we now know what's cooking, and that means..."

"...Let's go to sleep now because we won't do it tonight."

“You really enjoy completing what I say.”

"You're starting to get predictable, watch out!" Tecmov chuckled.

The two kids went back inside to get some rest, but they didn't know that someone else was about to do the same. And that someone had heard them.

That night, the duo rendezvoused atop the estate's roof. Tecmov had brought with him a strange contraption.

_"Don't tell me you had it in your suitcase"_, Entity whispered.

_"Exactly. I'm not the type to quit my job so easily, but since you think differently, here's how we'll resolve this issue without alarming the other guests, thus allowing us to finish our vacation after tonight."_

Tecmov opened the box, revealing a curious contraption covered in antennas, with a rather crude appearance.

_"This is the prototype for a kinetic shield that I'm working on these days. It can generate an energy blanket around the entire estate, and since I designed it to defend the glass walls of the MasterBase, it is also sound-absorbing."_

Tecmov activated the device.

_"Not a single vibration can pass through it, which is why no one will notice anything."_

_"But how long have you tested it?"_ Entity was worried by the appearance of the object.

_"It would already be installed on the MasterBase, if only you hadn't dragged me here."_

A blue glow came from the device, pouring onto the ground like a waterfall. Half a second, and it was completely gone.

_"...Did it work?"_

"Of course. Light passes through it unchanged, so it is invisible to the naked eye. At least, it is so as long as it is not attacked."

Tecmov lay down on the tiles.

"And now, you wait."

Ninety minutes later, a car appeared on the road. Four individuals emerged, one of whom was carrying a suitcase. They were all armed.

“Who had the brilliant idea to have them dress in white for a night operation?” Tecmov chuckled.

"They've been taught well," Entity observed. "If any of the survivors see them walking away, they will think they are from MasterBase, which will reinforce the charade."

"But think about it."

_"Silence, here they come."_

The two crouched behind the dormer window, while the individuals approached the door.

One of them took out a laser, but when he held out his hand to pick the lock, he was suddenly thrown away, while a strange light spread from the point where his hand had touched the field.

The other assassins looked around, disoriented, but it was too late: Entity's blade was already flashing above them.

The head of one blew off, but as Entity hit the ground to attack again, the others had already drawn their weapons.

"Stop where you are!" Entity had a gun pointed at him, but he wasn't the only one.

A bluish flash crossed the assassin's arm. then another pierced his head from ear to ear. Tecmov skidded from the roof, blaster in hand.

Well, two left, Entity denoted. One had drawn a foil, and was charging at Entity.

"On your guard!" The enemy was challenging him, and Entity wasn't about to say no.

Good form, Entity admitted, as they dueled. Too much shape, Entity sneered.

In a quick L-shaped motion, Entity's flat blade cut cleanly through his opponent's thin blade.

“The guard does little without a blade attached,” Entity grinned as she pierced his chest.

"Hey Tecmov, did you fix the other-" Entity turned to his companion, and saw him collapsed on the ground, convulsing.
The last remaining assassin towered over him. As he pulled out the knife, Entity noticed the aura in his gaze.

"Shit, a Mind-Bender!" Entity removed the sword from the victim's body and launched the attack.

But the muscles didn't respond.

Can it block two people at the same time? Lying on the ground, Entity was panicking.

"You two are the shittiest kind of people I've ever seen." With two mental checks in progress, the hitman struggled to coordinate, but that certainly wouldn't stop him from ripping Tecmov's back open. And then, to Entity.

"Now die in peace and let me receive the full reward."

Entity was starting to lose consciousness. His ears were ringing, and his eyes were closing. But with his last glimpse, he noticed that the same was happening to the hitman.

"What is it? I can't feel anything anymore, HELP!" The assassin was no longer in control of himself, and the Mind-Bending had given way to a very powerful hiss, which clouded the senses of both Entity and Tecmov. However, they still managed to distinguish the shape of a mysterious individual approaching the Mind-Bender and decapitating him.

As the hissing disappeared, the two regained their senses, and were able to see more clearly the figure of their savior, who, having sheathed his sword, was gathering the bodies.

"And who would you be?" Entity was half dazed, but pretty sure it was all over. In fact, it almost seemed to him that he had already seen it somewhere.

"A lone vigilante, that's all. I heard from you yesterday afternoon, and thought you could use a hand."

"Well, without you we would be dead, so you might as well stop underestimating this 'hand' you gave us." Tecmov chuckled as he got back to his feet. "Can I ask what you used? I'm very curious."

The boy took out some cube-shaped devices from his pocket. "Ultrasonic emitters, they stun the opponent and allow you to act practically undisturbed. The only disadvantage is that I have to completely deprive myself of hearing with these," he said, indicating the headphones around his neck, "But I still maintain the use of my sight, which is not granted to my opponents."

"Intriguing." Tecmov's eyes were shining.

"And what will you do now?" Entity asked him as he helped him dispose of the carcasses.

"Eh, nothing special, probably. I'm starting to get bored of being a lone wolf. In fact, I was kind of hoping you'd let me join you. I heard you talk about your Space-Hall, it sounds cool."

Entity and Tecmov looked at each other, then nodded.

"I'd say you have all the cards in order, just one last thing..." Entity smiled. "What would your nom de guerre be?"

The boy looked up.

"Red Raider, at your service."
