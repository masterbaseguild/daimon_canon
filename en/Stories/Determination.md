His muscles were starting to fail.

His breathing was becoming more and more labored.

But what was collapsing more than anything else was his psyche.

Jey had been trying the same spell for four hours. Nephasto had given him an extremely banal task: generate a beam of light powerful enough to sever a cable.

And there the cable was, still completely intact, as if it were protected by an impenetrable barrier.

Why am I doing this? Who made me start this thing? Was it really necessary?

It was probably the thirtieth time Jey had asked himself the same sequence of questions, in the exact same order.

Concentration.

Concentration.

Imagine a spark appearing in front of the palm of your hand.

Channel energy.

Materialize the image.

Here it is! At least I can get this far with good consistency.

Now, for the umpteenth time, expand.

Back in the dark.

He couldn't get any further, no matter how hard he tried. The exercise theoretically involved only a psychic effort, but Jey had had his arms outstretched in front of him for so long that he no longer felt his fingers.

Despair. A desperation never felt before. Or had this already happened to him in the past? Even if it was, it had been so long since the last time.

With each spark, his sword shone leaning on the stool. He watched him. he was tempting him.

Do I really need this thing? No, I don't need it. I was one of Olympus' best swordsmen, why am I here wasting time?

But once again, Jey's pride wouldn't let him.

Giving up was not an option. He had to do it.

But even if I succeeded, what use would I be to MasterBase? Do they need a first-class fighter more, or a scrappy wizard?

Jey raised his arms again. He took a breath that seemed to last an eternity.

Well, the solution to this problem is very simple. I will become stronger with magic than I ever was with the sword.

Concentration. Let's continue.
