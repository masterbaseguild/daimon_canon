Garo got out of the car first, followed soon after by Bzz and Zbb.

The ringing of the convenience store bell was followed by the dark silhouettes of the three Frankys, who immediately headed towards the counter.

"Hey, Froar, how are we doing?" The shopkeeper, however, refused to meet Garo's gaze.

"I'm speaking to you, you better look at me." Garo's tone became more threatening.

Reluctantly, Froar looked up.

"Look, I remember well that I owe you something, but business hasn't been going very well these days. Give me time to get to the next season, and you'll see that everything will settle down, and I'll be able to pay you-"

"Don't even dream of telling the Franky family what to do or not to do," Garo cut in. "It doesn't add up, and something has to change. So, here we are. Now, listen to me. If you don't pay, you officially become dead weight. And do you know what happens to dead weight?" A nod followed, at which Zbb cracked his knuckles.

"Please, I beg you, I only need a couple of weeks, it's really so close..." Garo noticed that the shopkeeper was barely holding back his tears. Then, out of the corner of his eye, he noticed Bzz was walking around the counter.

A punch straight to the stomach followed. Froar collapsed to the ground, retching.

Then, Bzz grabbed him by the scruff of the neck, and threw him across the counter. The poor Gondran fell at the feet of Garo.

Zbb kicked Froar's face, hurting his ear. More kicks followed, and more punches, as Bzz had also jumped over the counter.

Garo was paralyzed. Wasn't this supposed to be a simple persuasion visit? Was it really necessary? He noticed that he was losing his composure. Between one blow and another, Bzz looked at him, perplexed.

Garo then, terrified, threw himself at Froar, clenching his fists as hard as he could. But with each blow, his strength left him. With each stroke, that question became more and more imposing.

Who was Garo?
