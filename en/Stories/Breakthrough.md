"Hey Nepha, what are you doing?"

"Hi Beat, nothing new, but I still don't understand how this thing works."

The MasterBase library was messier than usual: papers strewn about, stacks of tomes on every desk, and a couple of magic projectors straight from the lab.

"But did you sleep last night?"

"Of course yes, but I woke up early to continue my studies. I'm going to go crazy, you have no idea."

"Tell me what's happening."

"Do you remember how I was studying the effects of Lampe's aura in the ether?"

"Wait, I don't remember all the modern theories of hetereology by heart."

"It's practically like a law of gravitation of the aether. The sources of magic are aware of the existence of other sources of magic present nearby, and behave accordingly with still unpredictable effects."

"Aaah yes, I see what you're referring to. And are you trying to determine the effects of this aura?"

"It's what I'd like to do, but I'm stuck before I even started."

"How come?"

"I need to place the study sample in an environment completely free of sources of magic, then introduce them in a controlled manner and observe the effects," Nephasto sighed dejectedly, "But no matter how many precautions I take, the study sample is not in perfect balance. I have drained the gravestones, activated a darkener to mask the presence of my soul, and made sure that I am the only person in the room when I conduct the tests. Obviously there are no other objects magical within the environment, apart from the gem I use as a specimen, yet its ethereal image does not stabilize."

Beat stopped to think for a few seconds, while Nephasto continued to leaf through the pages of yet another text in frustration.

"So," Beat began, "if you've stripped yourself of all other sources of magic in the environment, there must be something wrong with the ones you still have within it. Your soul, the obscurantor, or the gem."

"But which one could it be? The gem contains a spell so basic that all its characteristics are known by now, and as for the obscurantor, it should be a perfect mirror of the energy emanating from a sentient soul in the ether, which is why my soul and the obscurantor should cancel each other out from the gem's point of view."

"But there is a difference, you are a living being, and the obscurantor is not."

"But in the aether this makes no difference, the soul and biological life are two separate things, and the obscurant is made by separating a soul from a living body, then mirroring its energy. Other than this modification, my soul and the soul contained in the obscurant have the same shape."

“What if something else besides the soul was connected to a human body?”

Nephasto froze in place.

“Something that holds the soul and body together?” As he spoke these words, Nephasto jumped up, motioning for Beat to follow him.

"I don't know, just something that leaves the body along with the soul during the process of creating an obscurator, but is not captured because we are unaware of its existence."

“This could change a lot of things.” Nephasto was now climbing to the top of a shelf.

“Did you have an intuition?” Beat asked him.

"Perhaps." Nephasto replied, pulling a tome from the top shelf.

"And maybe it's something much bigger than just extending a theory from last year. We're going to need help."
