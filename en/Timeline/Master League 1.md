# Master League 1: The Interrupted Championship

**Master League 1** represents the very first official experiment aimed at establishing a regulated competitive league on an inter-regional and city scale within the Masters community. Designed as an elite tournament with matches focused on the **2v2 (Doubles)** formula, the event never reached its natural conclusion, being abruptly **cancelled due to the imminent outbreak of war hostilities** that would have shaken the sectors.

---

# I. The Context and Format

In the wake of the success of the old [[en/ClanWars|ClanWars]] organized by Aledge, MasterBase decided to promote a competition that not only involved the stellar macro-guilds, but also the individual territorial districts, the motorcycle clubs (MC) and the city federations born on Aura and in the territories of influence.

The format involved direct clashes in pairs ($2\text{v}2$), forcing participants to develop a perfect elementary or ballistic tactical synergy to overwhelm the opposing duo within the simulation arena.

# III. The Erasure and the Impact on Lore

Despite the enormous media coverage and the preparation of the city districts, Master League 1 failed to decide a winner. In the midst of the first group stages, the sudden escalation of violence linked to the Young Fighters' terrorist movements and the start of the **First Master War** maneuvers forced Entity to freeze the competition.

All the questors and leaders of the various clubs (including Entity and Shalida) were immediately called to arms to defend their respective quadrants, leaving the League trophy unawarded.

> ### ── Historical Note ──
> Although it failed in the bud, this tournament demonstrated for the first time the ability of the MasterBase to unite very distant civil, military and territorial factions under a single competitive aegis. Structural data from Master League 1 will be recovered years later to lay the foundation for future post-Redemption MasterBase Official Arms Leagues and Championships.
