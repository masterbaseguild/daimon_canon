# Coup d'état: The Isolation of the Commander

The **Coup** defines one of the darkest, most tense and structurally critical periods in the internal history of the [[en/Cast/Groups/MasterBase/MasterBase|MasterBase]]. Born from the psychological cracks opened by [[en/The First Wound|The First Wound]]'s recent cyber disaster, the event depicts the progressive ideological distancing between **Entity** and the rest of the guild, culminating in a full-blown political mutiny and the start of the supreme leader's iconic **lone vigilante** arc.

---

# I. The Prelude: The Silence of Entity

Following Rattly's cyber breach and the massive StarBase rebuild, Entity's emotional stability suffers a drastic collapse. Tormented by the certainty that the Alpha Sphere is now infiltrated by sleeper agents of the Xadrium Corporation or by independent saboteurs, the commander falls into a spiral of profound **geopolitical paranoia**.

When Entity intercepts the first traces of an imminent internal plot aimed at overthrowing the command of the Masters, he makes a radical and fatal choice: **he says nothing to anyone**. Convinced that no member of the crew possesses the coolness or skill needed to help him unravel such a web of deceit, and fearing that even his most trusted advisors may have been corrupted, Entity begins to move in the shadows, secreting reports and altering flight logs without the council's approval.

---

# II. The Breakdown of Trust and Mutiny

Entity's mysterious, elusive and blatantly evasive behavior does not go unnoticed. An air of strong tension begins to reign between the corridors and command bridges of the MasterBase: to all the questors and veteran officers (including the new post-Young Fighters additions) it seems clear that their leader is hiding something enormous and potentially dangerous for the safety of the station.

The lack of transparency is interpreted by the war council not as a security measure, but as a **sign of mental instability or, worse, as the beginning of institutional betrayal**.

To prevent Entity's unpredictable conduct from dragging the MasterBase into a new stellar cataclysm, a coalition of vanguard officers decides to act. Taking advantage of a moment of absence of the commander from the main rooms, the council carries out a **soft political coup**:
* Entity priority access keys are temporarily revoked from the ARC system.
* The leader is formally removed from supreme command of the fleet.
* Entity is ousted from the strategic decisions of the MasterBase, reduced to the rank of monitored guest within his own quarters.

---

# III. The Lone Vigilante Bow

Adamantly refusing to bow to what he sees as an oblivious and short-sighted rebellion on the part of his men, Entity decides to cut ties. Exploiting an old, untraceable analog backdoor in the base's systems, the commander escapes from his captivity, steals a cutting-edge light fighter and **permanently abandons the MasterBase, disappearing into the cosmic void**.

Thus began the era of the **Lone Vigilante**. Deprived of the logistical, economic and military support of his guild, Entity sheds the role of the rigid institutional commander to return to his origins as a front-line Questor:
* He moves in the shadows of the Alpha Sphere slums and frontier planets under an assumed name.
* Adopts brutal, hasty and unethical methods to extort information from mercenaries and informants linked to the Franky and Xadrium.
* He wages a private, silent war against the cell of saboteurs who were orchestrating the real coup, eliminating targets one at a time, like a ghost among the sectors.

---

# IV. Consequences and Status Quo

> ### ── Master Archive Register ──
> During Entity's absence, MasterBase experiences fragmented leadership lacking true messianic direction, understanding the hard way the complexity of the threats their former commander was attempting to stem in total solitude.

The lone vigilante arc will induce a profound character mutation in Entity, transforming him into a cynical, steely leader and profoundly detached from emotional dynamics. This painful transition — and the subsequent discovery by the guild of the true extent of the plots foiled by Entity alone — will lay the foundations for his future, dramatic return to the throne, a return that will take place under the sign of absolute submission and military respect on the part of the entire crew.
