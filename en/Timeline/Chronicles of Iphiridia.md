# Chronicles of Iphiridia: The Plague and Doom of Archen

The **Iphiridia Campaign** (also known in historical archives as the *Iphiridia Disaster*) was a massive military planetary cleanup operation sponsored by the **Olympus Guild** and coordinated on the ground by the **Nexus Guild**. The initiative, aimed at eradicating a thousand-year-old zombie plague, turned into a tactical cataclysm for the **Anarchist Squad (Anark)**, marking one of the most dramatic and dark pages in their history due to heavy losses and mysterious geopolitical sabotage.

---

# I. Genesis of the Campaign and Preparations

The planet **Iphiridia** had been in a state of total quarantine for centuries, devastated by a necrotic disease that had converted the entire population into infected and hyper-aggressive creatures.

### 1. The Olympus Ban
The elite guild Olympus, taking advantage of its dense network of interstellar contacts, intercepts the disinfestation program promoted by the Nexus Guild. The Nexus offered an advantageous deal: all affiliated guilds that participated in the cleansing would receive as a reward **colonial rights and ownership of a portion of the cleansed territories**.

### 2. Anark Mobilization and Recruitment
**John Drastic** officially forwards the invitation to the Anarch Squad. Aware that the guild's current forces would not be enough to face an entire infected planet, the leaders launch an emergency recruitment campaign:
* **Entity** and **Nephasto** enlist the Questors **Vlad**, **Cyr**, **Simon** and **Exitium**.
* **Wall** (Entity's brother) brings fighters **Miky** and **Radik** into the ranks.

Once the crew is complete, Olympus organizes a direct interstellar journey. Having landed on Iphiridia, the Anarchists found a fortified high-tech colony, naming it **Archen** (or *Arken*), using it as a bridgehead to start systematic extermination operations of the hordes.

---

# II. Political Intrigue: The Tattooed, Xadrium and the Betrayal of Garo

While the cleanup proceeds successfully and the Archen colony expands, the operation is compromised by external infiltration.

During a resupply expedition at the main Nexus Guild Hall, **Wolf** notices a combined team of agents from the **Xadrium Corporation** and the **Franky** underworld clan. Some of these individuals present a disturbing element: an **eye-shaped tattoo on the neck**, a symbol linked to old warnings from the Grand Magister.

The shady figures were orchestrating a real **blackmail against Nexus**: claiming to possess the definitive biological cure for the planet, they demanded concessions and structural favors. Smelling something burning, Nexus refuses, triggering diplomatic hostility from the blackmailers.

The Anark bursts into the room and engages in a fierce battle. In the midst of the melee, **Garo**, one of the vanguard fighters of the Franky faction, surprisingly decides to betray his employers, striking his own allies and helping the Anarchists put the Xadrium commando to flight. Following the victory, Garo pledges allegiance to the Anark, officially joining the guild.

---

# III. The Collapse and the Dramatic Retreat

Xadrium and the Frankys' failure to blackmail causes an immediate and catastrophic retaliation on the planet. In the space of a few days, **the zombie disease undergoes a sudden and artificial mutation**: the creatures increase exponentially in aggression, speed and tactical coordination.

The infected hordes descend en masse against population centers, eradicating and decimating one colony after another. Noting the impossibility of containing the new wave, the Nexus launches the alarm of **immediate general retreat**, recalling the Anark to the evacuation base to return home.

The retreat from Archen turns into a bloodbath:
* Evacuation shuttles are besieged by thousands of infected.
* To allow the rescue pods to take off and civilians to escape, numerous Anark members make the ultimate sacrifice on the front lines. Among the confirmed fallen, the warriors **Bruin** and **PK** stand out.
* **The fate of Vlad:** During the chaos of the escape, the Anark is forced to abandon **Vlad** on the field, believed dead under the weight of the horde. In reality, the Questor miraculously manages to survive, but remains **stranded alone on Iphiridia for years**, forced into a brutal fight for survival on a ghost planet before he can be tracked down.

---

# IV. Historical impact

> ### ── Consequences of the Campaign ──
> The Disaster of Iphiridia represented the nadir of the Anark's military career in the pre-Master era. The loss of Archen, the draining of the Nexus' resources and the psychological trauma of human losses (especially the presumed death of Vlad) opened a deep internal crisis within the guild, pushing Entity and the survivors to radically reconsider their alliances and security protocols against the Xadrium Corporation.
