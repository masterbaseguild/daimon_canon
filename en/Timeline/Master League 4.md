# Summoners League (Master League 4)

The **Summoners League**, officially cataloged in the archives as **Master League 4**, represented the most ambitious and complex attempt to standardize the war competition within the [[en/Cast/Groups/MasterBase/MasterBase|MasterBase]]. Abandoning the 2v2 (doubles) format used in the early leagues, the Summoners League introduced **5v5 (team)** combat, requiring an unprecedented level of tactical coordination, elementary synergies and field positioning.

---

# I. The Tactical Revolution: The 5v5 Format

The introduction of the 5v5 format marked the transition from simple skill duels to real "tactical maneuver" clashes. In this league, the brute strength of a single elite fighter was no longer enough; each team had to balance specific roles:
* **Auric Support:** Specialists in maintaining barriers and altering energy frequencies.
* **Heavy Vanguard:** The "tanks" responsible for absorbing enemy fire and creating gaps.
* **Rapid Assault:** Agile fighters aimed at eliminating high-value targets.

---

# II. The Dominion of the Romitas

The competition was dominated far and wide by the faction known as the **Romita**. Thanks to a natural understanding, studied obsessively over months of training in the base's simulation rooms, the Romitas imposed an oppressive pace of play.

Their style was based on a "rotation of positions" which sent opposing defenses into crisis. While the other clans were still trying to understand the logic of team positioning, the Romitas had already transformed every match into a game of chess, closing the combat series with pinpoint precision that left MasterBase observers amazed.

---

# III. Suspension for Resource Reallocation

Despite the public success and the prestige that the league was giving to the participating teams, the Summoners League suffered the same fate as the first historical experiment.

Following a sudden sector-wide war emergency—which required the **massive reallocation of energy resources and simulation systems** to power StarBase's active defenses and border patrols—the Entity council decreed the **immediate cancellation of the tournament**.

> ### ── Service Note ──
> The closure of the Summoners League was viewed with extreme disappointment by the Romitas, who saw themselves deprived of the possibility of mathematically sanctioning their total domination with the final victory. However, the decision was necessary: ​​the energy required to keep the 5v5 holographic arenas running suddenly became vital to ensuring the operation of the base's reactors during the tensions preceding Entity's return from exile.

---

# IV. Inheritance

The Summoners League remains in the archives as the "unfinished league of tactical geniuses". The supremacy of the Romita in this brief but intense glimpse of time has become a recurring myth in the narratives of veteran Questors, demonstrating how the MasterBase had now reached a degree of martial complexity superior to any other guild in the Alpha Sphere. The tactics tested in this league were subsequently archived in the central database, ready to be reused in the years to come.
