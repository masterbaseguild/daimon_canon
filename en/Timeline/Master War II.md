# War with SpaceHub: The Beginning of the Second War Master

The **War with the SpaceHub** constitutes one of the bloodiest and most decisive chapters of the **Second Master War**. This conflict, born from the ashes of the MadHeadz betrayal, represents the first practical application of the new [[en/Cast/Groups/MasterBase/MasterBase|MasterBase]] military doctrine: preventive war. The clash saw the total destruction of the emerging faction led by **Blaze**, redefining the balance of power within the MasterVerse.

---

# I. The Context and Rise of Blaze

Following the dissolution and fragmentation of the historic macro-guild **WorldOrder**, many of its leading officers chose the path of independence to re-establish their own private fiefdoms. Among these, the warrior **Blaze** quickly stood out as one of the most charismatic, ambitious and unscrupulous figures in the sector.

With the aim of centralizing political and technological power in his own quadrants, Blaze founded the **SpaceHub** guild, gathering mercenaries, renegades and former members of old coalitions around him. SpaceHub's rapid rise and Blaze's calculating influence began to arouse strong suspicion among the veteran ranks, particularly among the former members of **SpaceFox** (now merged with MasterBase), who openly frowned upon and distrusted him since the days of the old order.

---

# II. The False Alliance and Technological Opportunism

To accelerate the militarization of his guild, Blaze used a fine diplomatic strategy, proposing a non-aggression and cooperation treaty at the MasterBase.

Still vulnerable due to recent logistical restructuring, the Masters initially accepted the partnership. Blaze immediately took advantage of this, **exploiting the communication channels and laboratories of the MasterBase to drain energy resources, sensitive data and latest generation advanced technologies**.

However, SpaceHub's purely opportunistic and parasitic intentions did not remain hidden for long. The movements of the Blaze fleets became speculative and the data flows intercepted by the StarBase surveillance systems confirmed the worst: SpaceHub was accumulating technological power for the sole purpose of turning it, at the right moment, against the Masters themselves.

---

# III. Preventive Choice and Conflict

Marked by the burning betrayal suffered by the MadHeadz, the MasterBase leadership decided not to repeat the same mistake. Instead of passively awaiting Blaze's final about-face, Entity and the war council interpreted SpaceHub's opportunistic behavior as a direct and imminent existential threat.

Applying the maxim of *"strike first to avoid being hit"*, MasterBase **officially declared all-out pre-emptive war on SpaceHub**, mobilizing its entire combat fleet.

The conflict was of devastating intensity. Blaze, surprised by the fury and speed of the Masters' offensive - which gave SpaceHub no time to organize a coordinated defensive line - took refuge in his headquarters.

---

# IV. The Fall of SpaceHub and Consequences

The final act of the war took place in the heart of the SpaceHub sector. By coordinating the flagship's main weapons systems with ground assault team incursions, the MasterBase breached the enemy's last ring of defense.

The clash ended with a ruthless verdict: **SpaceHub headquarters was completely bombed and razed to the ground**. With the destruction of its technological stronghold and the dispersion of the survivors, the guild definitively ceased to exist as an autonomous entity within the MasterVerse.

> ### ── Lore Consequences ──
> The total cancellation of SpaceHub consolidated MasterBase's reputation as a ruthless superpower unyielding against any form of diplomatic duplicity. Blaze lost its power base, and the outcome of this preemptive war swept away any remaining illusions of peace among the remaining great guilds, marking the point of no return in the Second Master War.
