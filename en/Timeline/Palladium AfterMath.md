# Shinya vs Franky

After the victory of the Third Master War, the team agreed that a break was more than deserved. Some of the Masters had taken a vacation on aura, while others remained on MasterBase, continuing to carry out operations from time to time, but without embarking on any particularly large-scale adventures.
One day, a Gondran arrived at MasterBase and asked to speak to Emily, claiming to be an old acquaintance of hers. It was suspicious, but Emily showed up anyway.
At first, the gondran claimed that he too was a fugitive from the Royal Family, having come to MasterBase to seek asylum. Emily struggled to remember his identity, but she was sure she had seen him before, which is why she believed him.
The two continued talking, and the more time passed, the more disturbing the conversation became, and Emily became alarmed. Suddenly, the gondran said "the franky family knows no treachery" and pulled out a pistol, but before he could pull the trigger, Garo's harpoon flashed into the room, piercing the gondran's hand, and disarming him.
After having immobilized the hitman, and after the whole MasterBase had arrived running, Garo explained that having become suspicious, he had observed the conversation from the cameras, and after a while he had recognized the mysterious gondran as a member of the Franky Family. He then explained that the reason Emily hadn't been able to fully recognize him, and why Garo had needed more time to do so, was that the hitman had already lost the dark complexion that asteroid radiation causes. When Garo asked him why, the assassin replied that he had now been stationed on the planet Zawa for three months, after the Franky family had finally conquered the Gondraw system.
The Masters were astounded by the news, and tried to get more information from the hitman, but he pulled out his Gondran tentacle, freed himself and launched himself into the assault on the nearest armed Master, thus being killed. Entity remarked that his intent was probably to commit suicide.
The Masters agreed that, although it was first necessary to obtain confirmation of the claims made by the hitman, it was necessary to move as soon as possible, and that the peace was over.
