# Operation MasterCon: The Baptism of Fire

**Operation MasterCon** defines one of the most significant and educational military campaigns in the history of the [[en/Cast/Groups/MasterBase/MasterBase|MasterBase]]. Set entirely on the planet Aura in the post-[[en/Timeline/Master War I|first war master]], the operation marks the guild's first attempt to expand onto the planetary surface, culminating in a catastrophic strategic defeat against the superpower **Asgard** that drastically scaled back Entity's ambitions.

---

# I. The Prelude: The Pact with Olympus

Strengthened by the overwhelming victory against the Young Fighters, morale on board the MasterBase is sky high. The collective euphoria and the newly formed fusion with the SpaceFox veterans push the command to take the next step: **establish a fixed colony on the surface of the planet Aura**.

To obtain permits and logistical support, Entity turns to the leaders of the **Olympus Foundation**. The super-guild proposes a mutual defense agreement:
* MasterBase would provide its cutting-edge war support to Olympus to **recapture a strategic city** that had fallen under the control of the Asgard Empire.
* In exchange for the success of the operation, Olympus promised to cede jurisdiction over an entire sector, dedicating an official land base to the Masters.

The joint assault is a total success. The city is liberated from enemy garrisons and MasterBase gets its land.
---

# II. The Act of Ibris and the Disaster of the *Sapphire Party*

Blinded by the double military success, the members of the MasterBase are guilty of pride and martial inexperience. Convinced that they are now invincible, the Masters propose a crazy plan to Olympus: **launch a total offensive against the deep heart of Asgard** to win the planetary war in one fell swoop.

Olympus, aware of the real war potential of the Empire, categorically refuses the initiative, warning the Masters about the risks of such an undertaking. Moved by youthful impetuosity, Entity and his companions decide to proceed completely alone.

The camp's verdict is merciless. Asgard deploys its legendary elite armies (the cast from the old comic) on the front lines. The Masters, structurally and tactically unprepared for a clash of this magnitude, suffer a **sounding and devastating defeat**. The event, dramatically remembered in internal records as the **Sapphire Party**, ends in a massacre in which MasterBase loses most of its men in the field.

> ### ── Command Log ──
>Back on the flagship, Entity falls into a state of deep despair, consumed by guilt for leading his companions to the slaughter. However, the crew rallies around their leader, comforting him and reminding him that the decision to attack Asgard was a collective approval of the guild, not an individual failure.

---

# III. Consequences, New Recruits and the Quark Mystery

Despite the defeat, the heroism demonstrated by the Masters in the field profoundly affected many of the Olympus soldiers and technicians who had fought alongside them in the first phase of the operation. As a sign of profound respect and solidarity, a real migration of talent occurs:

* **The New Masters:** Several Questors resign from Olympus to permanently join the MasterBase. Among these, the names of **Winter**, **Shane**, **Cimo**, **Duoco** and the veteran **Andreas** stand out.
* **Keyplexity's Support:** The allied guild *Keyplexity* takes to the field at an engineering level, providing the resources and materials necessary to repair the engines of the damaged StarBase and allow it to resume flight in the cosmos.
* **Jerome's Entrance:** During city operations, a young Aura native boy named **Jerome** naively meddles in the guild's affairs. Taking a liking to the crew for his courage, he is welcomed on board. Jerome will develop over the years as a formidable warrior; however, since the earthly *MasterHall* had not yet been founded, struck by nostalgia for his roots, he later decided to abandon the Masters to officially enter Olympus.

### The Encounter with Quark
During the Aura campaign, the Masters come into contact for the first time with an enigmatic Questor named **Quark**. This bond will prove tragic: years later, unable to overcome the traumas and psychological consequences resulting from the cataclysms and accidents that would have affected the sectors, Quark will die by suicide, leaving an indelible shadow in the memory of the guild.

---

# IV. Final Status Quo: Lesson Learned

Operation MasterCon ends with MasterBase and Olympus parting ways on good terms. Demonstrating a newfound logistical maturity, MasterBase temporarily refuses the offer of the Earth base on Aura promised by Olympus, admitting that it does not yet possess the forces and economic resources necessary to garrison and maintain a planetary outpost.

Entity promises that the guild will return to reclaim that territory only when it is truly ready, and Olympus solemnly promises to guard and return the base when the right time comes.

> ### ── Palladium Archives Note ──
> The elite warriors of Asgard who inflicted defeat on the Masters during the Sapphire Party will reappear years later, during the **Palladium** era, reduced to the ranks of minions and minor mercenaries in the pay of new global threats, facing a radically different and infinitely more lethal MasterBase.
