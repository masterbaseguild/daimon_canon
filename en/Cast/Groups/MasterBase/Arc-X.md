# ARC-X

**ARC-X** is an executive android of very high complexity, designed to serve as the physical interface and on-board computer of the [[en/Cast/Groups/MasterBase/MasterBase|MasterBase]]. It represents the embodiment of the "ARC Protocol", a holistic Guild management system that integrates superhuman computing capabilities with impeccable executive efficiency.

---

### I. Genesis and Development
The ARC-X project is the result of a stratification of engineering skills over the years, undergoing constant evolutions depending on its supervisor:
* **Dr. Held:** The original architect, the one who laid the logical and structural foundations of the android, conceiving it as the synthetic brain destined to guide the entire operation of the Masters.
* **Alex:** After Held's death, Alex took control of the project. Under his leadership, ARC-X achieved greater operational independence and ability to adapt in dynamic combat scenarios.
* **Alone:** Following Alex's passing, the baton was passed to Alone, who further refined the ARC-X protocols, making it not only the on-board computer, but an essential element in the management of tactical crises and the internal stability of the MasterBase.

---

### II. Functions and Role
ARC-X operates as a technological singularity within the Guild:
* **ARC Protocol Executor:** Manages data flows, perimeter security and logistics resources of the MasterBase.
* **Tactical Interface:** In conflict contexts, he can abandon the role of supervisor to take the field, acting as a shock force coordinated by the central system.
* **Historical Memory:** Having passed through multiple mentors, ARC-X has an archive of MasterBase decision-making that transcends the lives of individuals, making it the longest-lived witness to the Guild's evolution.

---

### III. Synthetic nature
Unlike other operators, ARC-X does not possess an autonomous will linked to personal ambitions: its existence is inextricably linked to the survival and efficiency of the MasterBase. However, its algorithmic complexity is such that the line between command execution and autonomous initiative is often blurred, resulting in an entity that many Guild members treat not as a tool, but as a sentient colleague.
