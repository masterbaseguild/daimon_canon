The MasterBase is a [[en/Glossary/Guild|guild]] of the [[en/Map/Alpha Sphere/Alpha Sphere|Alpha Sphere]], proprietary by [[en/Map/Alpha Sphere/Starbases/MasterBase|starbase of the same name]]. Its members, the **Masters**, are made up of warriors, engineers and researchers who dedicate their lives to maintaining order in the space sectors they traverse.

# History and Conflicts

## Foundation (2018)
The MasterBase was born in 2018 from Entity's desperate attempt to preserve what remained of the [[en/Cast/Groups/Disbanded/Anark|Anark]], decimated by the [[en/Timeline/Chronicles of Iphiridia|Iphiridia disaster]] and dissolved by Theo's will.

As the old guild faded away, Entity (in collaboration with Tecmov and [[en/Cast/Deceased/Olympus Foundation/Albert|Albert]]) began construction of a mammoth [[en/Glossary/StarBase|starbase]] orbital as the new headquarters. For the undertaking, Entity recruited the ex-Anark Wall, Emily and Nephasto, to which were added Tecmov, Mr. Jey (met at a [[en/Timeline/The Olympus Grand Tournament|tournament]]), Garo (rescued during the [[en/Timeline/Operation FrankyRose|Operation FrankyRose]]) and finally Theo himself.

Thus the [[en/Glossary/8 Founding Masters|8 founding masters]] were born. Once the station was put into orbit, the guild officially opened its doors to the public in June 2018, welcoming new recruits including Honda, Damashi, Red Raider, Black Paladin, Beat, Blu Wind, Proximus, Error, Davrin and Dr. Latency.

## The first council of the HeadMasters and the First Master War
The rapid growth of the guild led to the establishment of the HeadMasters, an administrative council composed of Entity, Garo, Mr. Jey, and Nephasto.

Soon the territorial expansion on small asteroids and minor planets attracted the hostility of the [[en/Cast/Groups/Disbanded/Young Fighters|YoungFighters]]. Feeling threatened, the rival faction adopted an aggressive policy that resulted in the [[en/Timeline/Master War I|first war master]]. During the year of conflict, MasterBase welcomed numerous disgruntled defectors from the Young Fighters, including Aledge, Chojiro, Disti, and Alone. The war ended when the AUN declared rival leader Loris an outlaw, forcing him to flee and dissolving his guild.

## The Second Council of the HeadMasters and the Plague
After a year, Garo and Nephasto left the council for more operational duties, being replaced by Beat, who had meanwhile built an excellent reputation. The new triumvirate (Beat, Entity, Mr. Jey) also led the guild during the difficult period of the Plague, during which new recruits joined: Shane, Cimo, Andreas, Duoco, Giuliz, Crafter and Winter.

## Sabotage and rebirth (The Leagues)
The guild's stability was compromised by [[en/Cast/Rattly|Rattly]], who managed to sneak into the new ARC system, developed by Disti, and destroy the MasterBase from within.

To rebuild the faction's influence, the **MasterBase Leagues** project was inaugurated. To support the new initiative, Flavia, Sakkana, Axox and Silver were recruited, also forming an alliance with the [[en/Cast/Groups/Disbanded/MiddleEarth|MiddleEarth]] faction. Despite sabotage by a band of pirates led by [[en/Cast/Deceased/Khight|Khight]], the guild recovered, later becoming involved in a second major Master War against SpaceHub.

## Political Instability and Schisms

Following particularly authoritarian reforms, Beat led a democratization movement that took command away from Entity, pushing him into voluntary exile. Under the new leadership, Beat, Eugene, Dave, and Nephasto formed a council to study magical field interference to gain military advantage.

Upon his return, Entity harshly hindered the Archivists' research, pushing them to abandon the guild altogether. Entity later imposed a further authoritarian regime. The discontent pushed the most anarchic wing of the members, led by Alone, to definitively separate from MasterBase.

# Structure and Culture

## Hierarchy
The guild's hierarchical order is structured as follows:
1. **OverMaster** (Supreme Leader)
2. **HeadMasters** (Board Members)
3. **ArcMasters**
4. **GrandMasters**
5. **Masters**

## Life aboard the Starbase
Masters spend much of their lives aboard the station, a geometric modular structure protected by omnidirectional shields. Its central module houses a warp drive and a gravity simulator integrated into the architecture. In addition to a large spaceport for the fleet, the interior is a truly self-sufficient city complete with gyms, entertainment areas, laboratories, canteens, offices and dormitories, where warriors train and researchers study new technologies and spells.

## Musical Culture
The guild has a deep musical tradition lived as a tool for camaraderie. Musical evenings animated by the Masters themselves are regularly held in the area known as **La Masteria**. The guild boasts an official anthem (*MasterBase Anthem*), space chants (*Shanties*) and traditional *Fabula Nova* medleys. The base intercoms themselves often broadcast Master Aledge's compositions.

# Official Competitions

To keep morale high and skills honed, MasterBase regularly organizes official competitions involving its members and, in some cases, external participants as well.

## Editions of the MasterBase Leagues

* **2019 Trainer League**: Won by the FPS team, composed of Lee.
* **2020 Fighter League**: Won by the LitCore team, consisting of Sakkana and Axox.
* **2020 Middle Earth Championship**: Won by the LitCore team, consisting of Sakkana and Axox.
* **2021 Summoner League**: Won by team Romita, consisting of Endemion, Frost, Noie, TreeRex and Surfy.

* **2023 Omega Stars Championship:** In collaboration with the Zenith guild, won by Kishiro, Vinc and Green.

## DeathMatch

Survival competitions used as training. The participants, divided into teams, are isolated in a wild biome where they must procure resources and eliminate each other for the trophy.
* **2020 Act of Rising DeathMatch:** The inaugural competition, won by Beat, Blu Wind and Tecmov.
* **2022 Golden Boar DeathMatch:** Held after a year's break to make room for Leagues and dedicated to the memory of *Technoblade*. Won by Alone, Hooneex and Mape.
* **2023 Summoning DeathMatch:** Won by Xyroh, Entity, and Tecmov.
* **2025 Reckoning DeathMatch:** The most recent edition, won by Alone, Mape and Winter.
