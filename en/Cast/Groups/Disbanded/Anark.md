The Anark was a historic [[en/Glossary/Guild|guild]] [[en/Map/Alpha Sphere/Talos System/Planet Aura/Planet Aura|aural]], originally born as a small group of provincial vigilantes and ended up becoming one of the key factions of the tragic disaster of Iphiridia. From its ashes the [[en/Cast/Groups/MasterBase/MasterBase|MasterBase]] was later born.

# History

## The Origins (2016)
The Anark was born almost by chance in 2016. Theo, just twelve years old at the time, managed to steal some advanced armaments from [[en/Cast/Groups/Olympus Foundation/Gavil|Gavil]], an operative of the [[en/Cast/Groups/Olympus Foundation/Olympus Foundation|Olympus]]. Theo distributes the stolen equipment to his closest friends—Eugene, PK, and Artifex—and together they found the Anark. The initial goal of the newly formed guild is simple and local: to act as vigilantes to finally restore order to their small town.

## The Olympus Expansion and Sponsorship (2017)
The following year, the group expanded with the entry of Entity, Wall and, shortly after, Nephasto. The arrival of Entity brings a revolutionary and ambitious breath within the Anark: his resourcefulness pushes his companions to embark on increasingly bigger and riskier missions.

This daring culminates when the guild accidentally finds itself involved in a violent skirmish between a team from Olympus and one from [[en/Cast/Groups/Asgard Foundation|Asgard]]. Following the accident, Gavil (who had taken part in the fight) recognizes the boys' potential and offers them to enter the Olympus sponsorship program. Convinced by Entity's enthusiasm, the group accepts. The Anark begins to be trained by the foundation's professionals, undergoing a drastic and rapid improvement in their warfare abilities.

## The Iphiridia Campaign (End 2017)
Towards the end of 2017, the guild [[en/Cast/Groups/Disbanded/Nexus|Nexus]] launches a desperate appeal to all the guilds of the [[en/Map/Alpha Sphere/Alpha Sphere|Alpha Sphere]] to free the [[en/Map/Alpha Sphere/Nobi System/Planet Iphiridia/Planet Iphiridia|Planet Iphiridia]] from a lethal anomaly known as "the Plague". Feeling ready, the Anark members decide to join the representative forces sent by Olympus for the [[en/Timeline/Chronicles of Iphiridia|business]].

To prepare for a military campaign of this magnitude, the guild begins a massive recruitment phase, drawing on the knowledge developed in the academy: thus Cyrus, Exitium, Mikyz, Vlad and Damashi join.
Having landed on Iphiridia, the Anark enters the infested territories and founds the outpost city of [[en/Map/Alpha Sphere/Nobi System/Planet Iphiridia/Arken|Arken]]. Using the settlement as a base of operations, the young [[en/Glossary/Quester|Questors]] wrest ground from the plague, conquering and cleaning up a decent province.

During this time, an Anark team on a supply mission to the Nexus capital ends up eavesdropping on a confidential conversation in the headquarters. The discovery leads to a violent scuffle with a group of mysterious individuals; during the clash, one of these individuals, a certain Garo, betrays his companions and deserts, joining the Anark.

## The Disaster and the Dissolution
Suddenly, the plague on Iphiridia mutates, dramatically increasing in aggression and breaching numerous coalition bases. Faced with the massacre, the Nexus is forced to declare the failure of the entire campaign and order a planetary evacuation.

However, when the order is finally issued, it is already too late: the plague has already invaded the Nexus headquarters. The evacuation turns into a desperate and chaotic route. Anark pays a very high blood price, losing almost half of its forces: the founders Artifex and PK, together with Cyrus, Exitium, Mikyz and Vlad, lose their lives in the massacre.

For the survivors (Theo, Eugene, Entity, Wall, Nephasto, Garo and Damashi) the blow is devastating. Once returned to the planet Aura, leader Theo decrees the immediate and definitive dissolution of the Anark. The decision is driven by deep resentment towards Entity, to whom Theo attributes moral responsibility for the tragedy for having pushed a simple group of provincial vigilantes far beyond their limits.

Despite the failure, Entity will take up the baton of this traumatic experience: starting from the core of the Anark survivors, he will give life to the MasterBase project, with the aim of succeeding where his old guild had failed.
