# Rattly

**Rattly** represents one of the darkest and most traumatic episodes in the recent history of **MasterBase**. Ex-[[en/Glossary/Quester|questor]] a high-level affiliate of the defunct **SpaceFox** guild, Rattly made history not for his frontier exploits, but for the devastating internal sabotage operation that nearly led to the guild's total collapse.

---

# I. Origins: The SpaceFox Legacy

Rattly's training is rooted in the cynical, high-tech culture of the **SpaceFox** guild. In that context, he developed digital manipulation and intrusion skills that, over time, evolved into a form of "chaos art". His move to MasterBase, initially seen as a prestige signing, turned out to be the guild's biggest recruiting error.

---

# II. Sabotage: "The Digital Eclipse"

Rattly's name is synonymous with the most serious internal breach ever suffered by the facility:

* **ARC Breach:** Using his technical skills, Rattly managed to bypass the security protocols of the **ARC** (the cyber and informational heart of the guild). He didn't just read data; injected a series of "logic viruses" designed to destabilize life support systems, portal navigation, and the structural integrity of the base.
* **Sabotage from Within:** As the digital defenses collapsed, Rattly took action physically, sabotaging the sustainment bays and hangars. His strategy was clear: paralyze the MasterBase, making it unable to respond, only to watch it implode under the weight of its own complexity.
* **Result:** The operation caused irreparable damage to several sectors of the station and sparked a climate of paranoia among the guild members that took months to dismantle.

---

# III. Profile and Motivations

* **Techniques:** Rattly is not a melee fighter; his "weapon" is knowledge of the protocols. He exploits the flaws in the system that he himself previously contributed to building.
* **Psychology:** Rattly is driven by a nihilistic view of the role of guilds. For him, MasterBase—with its pretense of order and control—was an artificial structure doomed to failure. His sabotage was a sort of "social experiment" aimed at demonstrating how fragile the power system built by the Questars was.

---

# IV. Current Status: Wanted

* **Elusiveness:** After the sabotage, Rattly managed to escape, taking advantage of the chaos he generated to disappear between the intersystem routes.
* **Class A Threat:** Currently considered one of MasterBase's priority internal security targets. His knowledge of the systems makes his hunt particularly difficult, as he is able to predict the countermeasures the guild employs to track him down.

> ### ── Archive Note (Security Protocol) ──
> *«Rattly didn't just want to steal data. He wanted to see if the architecture we had built could stand the weight of betrayal. He didn't hate us while he destroyed the base; he studied it. If he shows up again, don't try to negotiate. Rattly doesn't give up, he 'reconfigures' your intentions to his advantage.”* — Security advisory issued after the event.

---

# V. Narrative Notes
Rattly's betrayal marked the end of MasterBase's era of innocence. Since that moment, the procedures for accessing the ARC have been completely revised, and distrust towards former members of external guilds has become the norm, transforming the internal climate into a more guarded and paranoid one.
